Case study

INVIROTEL was appointed in 2012 to implement Phase 1 of a two phased project. Phase 1 was to implement an end-to-end smart metering solution in the
Rossenekal area (600 meters). Phase 2 was to implement an end-to-end smart metering solution in the entire municipal area subject to the success of Phase 1
(10 000 meters). Rossenekal town consists of 80% of RDP houses, residents were provided with free electricity for over 5 years.

Challenges Encountered:

  • Roossenekal is a remote rural area from the municipal office of Groblersdal.
  • Cellular network coverage is highly irregular.
  • No electricity suppliers for over 150 km.
  • For five years residents have not paid for electricity.
  • The local municipal staff lacked critical computer skills and training.
  • Municipal records of residences and points of delivery- non existent.
  • Previous failed installation of smart meters doubts among officials.
  • Work to be done on risk (payment to Invirotel was subjected to the successful implementation of Phase 1).

Implementation

  • Successfully implemented phase one 578 smart meters installed
  • FET college students trained and empoweredMunicipal staff are able to manage the meters, revenue collection and vending.
  • Residence have started paying electricity.
  • 100% recovery rate by the municipality.No protest or complaints from community.
  • Selling electricity at a profit.Most sophisticated electricity metering infrastructure in Limpopo.
  • Phase two awarded consisting of over 12000 meters.
